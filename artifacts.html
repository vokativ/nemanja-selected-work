Company Data + AI Platform

Turned a stalled analytics effort into the daily operating layer for subscription revenue, trial take-up, churn, sales, logistics, support, and product usage.

Private case study
Problem

Subscription performance was effectively opaque. Trial take-up, renewal behavior, revenue movement, churn reasons, channel performance, and usage patterns were almost impossible to understand daily; teams often needed quarterly-style reviews to reconstruct what had happened.

Built

I stabilized failing VM-based ingestion and orchestration, expanded the warehouse from raw engineering database copies into business domains, then rebuilt the stack on Terraform-managed GCP, GKE, Cloud Composer, BigQuery, Dataform, Airbyte, Superset, Cloud Functions, and Cloud Run.

Judgment

The work was not just modernization. It required rescuing broken syncs after schema changes, keeping critical data moving with temporary Python notebook extraction, replacing fragile paths with Cloud Functions and Airflow DAGs, and handling messy details such as currency normalization and exchange-rate sourcing for billing analytics.

Impact

Leaders and teams could finally manage subscription performance actively instead of discovering problems late. Daily visibility into trial conversion, revenue, churn, product usage, sales, support, and logistics became part of the business operating rhythm.

Golf Genie: Conversational Analytics

Internal production AI analytics app that helped leadership, product, and non-technical teams ask complex business questions without starting from a blank SQL prompt.

AI analytics
Problem

Important product and subscription questions were trapped behind dashboards, SQL, or analyst backlogs. Business users needed a faster way to ask questions such as renewal and usage behavior by customer vintage, month, and geography.

Built

A FastAPI + Gemini analytics app on Cloud Run, connected to curated BigQuery datasets and designed for internal use by leadership, product, and any team that needed data answers.

Judgment

The app was more than a chatbot connected to a database. It used several dozen analyst-verified query patterns, showed the SQL being used, and made CSV/Excel exports part of the workflow so business follow-up with analysts started from concrete evidence.

Impact

Teams could explore business questions in plain English while still preserving transparency. The generated SQL and exports reduced the friction of moving from a first answer to deeper analysis.

Company Office Metrics Display

A live TV-native dashboard that gave offices a shared view of what was happening across products, channels, customers, support, logistics, and regions.

Live operations
Problem

Company results were siloed. Teams did not have a shared, ambient view of product usage, sales, support signals, logistics movement, or customer feedback.

Built

A Bun + TypeScript app using Canvas 2D, WebSockets, and BigQuery-backed refreshes. It was built for office TVs, including Amazon Silk on Fire TV, while still scaling from mobile screens to 4K displays.

Judgment

The dashboard treated display context as a product constraint. URL parameters controlled units and behavior, so North American teams could see imperial units while other offices saw metric defaults.

Impact

The display made company activity visible without asking every team to dig through reports. It also surfaced unusual changes and helped teams understand the operating picture across functions.

Bun, TypeScript, Canvas 2D, WebSockets BigQuery-backed metrics TV, mobile, and 4K layouts

Signals included

Product usage: activity by product, region, and behavior.
Sales and logistics: Shopify, third-party channels, Amazon, shipping, and geography.
Customer voice: CSAT, churn surveys, and Gemini-assisted free-text categorization into actionable reason groups.
Shared display: live office screen for visibility, alignment, and lightweight operational alerting.

hocudaglasam.com

A public civic-tech product that simplified Serbian diaspora voter registration while keeping sensitive personal data on the user's device.

Public project
Problem

Serbian citizens abroad faced a clumsy registration process: finding the right embassy, downloading and printing forms, filling them manually, scanning documents, and sending everything to the correct address.

Built

An open-source Angular/TypeScript app that generated the registration document client-side and helped users route the completed materials to the relevant embassy or consulate.

Judgment

The core product decision was privacy by architecture. The app did not need server-side PII collection; sensitive data stayed on the user's device, and the code was public so citizens and institutions could inspect the behavior.

Impact

The project became public enough to attract institutional scrutiny and press coverage, which made transparency and clear privacy communication as important as the product workflow itself.

GitHub repo BBC Serbian interview Danas coverage

Privacy-aware workflow

User enters data: the browser has the information needed to generate the form.
Client-side document: the registration output is produced on the user's device.
User sends materials: the citizen sends the generated document and passport image to the relevant embassy or consulate.
Public code: open-source implementation allows inspection of the no-server-PII model.

Adiufant

An active AI calendar assistant for busy parents, built around the daily mess of school notices, images, PDFs, voice notes, and preparation tasks.

Active build
Problem

Family logistics often arrive as fragmented inputs: WhatsApp messages, school PDFs, photos, forms, voice notes, and reminders that are easy to miss until too late.

Built

An AI assistant concept and early product that converts multimodal inputs into structured calendar events and preparation tasks using Gemini.

Judgment

The product is designed around a real workflow rather than a generic assistant: extract the event, identify what needs to be prepared, and make the next action visible before the parent has to remember it.

Status

This is intentionally framed as an active build, not a mature production system. It is useful proof of ongoing AI product thinking and hands-on shipping beyond private company work.
