Company Data + AI Platform

Turned a stalled analytics effort into the daily operating layer for subscription behavior, revenue, trial take-up, payments, sales, logistics, support, product usage, and executive company-health reporting.

Private case study
Sizing the problem Core metrics took 1-2 weeks and 3-5 people to procure, while some business questions could not be answered at all.
Business impact Daily visibility across trials, subscriptions, payments, revenue, usage, support, logistics, and product behavior.
Operating leverage Reduced manual reporting loops, brittle finance reconstruction, and recurring SKU/order-sync escalations.

Problem

Many core operating metrics were slow, manual, fragmented, or impossible to report. Usage, trial take-up, subscription behavior, payment movement, sales performance, support signals, and product usage could take 1-2 weeks and 3-5 people to procure, while some questions were not answerable at all.

Impact

Leaders and teams could finally manage the business actively instead of reconstructing it late. Daily visibility into trial conversion, subscription behavior, payment movement, revenue, product usage, sales, support, and logistics became part of the operating rhythm, helping management steer a major operating performance turnaround.

Reset a failed support AI procurement, replaced a misfit tooling setup, and turned support interactions into earlier product and engineering signals.

Practical AI

Problem

A customer-service AI solution had been bought without the operating work needed to train, maintain, and improve it. The contract was substantial, the system was underperforming, and support teams were still buried in manual work.

Built

Security, Governance, and Production Discipline

Made the data and AI operating layer more enterprise-ready through SSO, backups, least-privilege access, governed BI, and safer production operations.

Enterprise trust
Risk surface Analytics and AI systems touched company, customer, billing, and athlete data.
Business continuity 100+ users kept access through the Superset migration with dashboards and connections preserved.
Enterprise trust SSO, backups, governed BI, least-privilege IAM, and key cleanup made the platform safer to operate.
AI readiness Made self-service analytics and AI workflows safer by fixing the trust layer underneath them.
Problem

Production analytics and AI systems touched company, customer, billing, and athlete data. The inherited setup had fragile VM deployments, stale access, scattered keys, limited disaster recovery, and overly broad permissions.

Built

I migrated Superset from a VM to Kubernetes with Microsoft 365 SSO, automated backups, custom roles, and preserved existing dashboards and users. I also tightened GCP access, service accounts, API keys, and dataset-level BigQuery permissions.

Judgment

Enterprise AI and analytics only work when people can trust the operating layer. The goal was reproducibility, access control, recovery, and safe self-service, not just dashboards or prototypes.

Impact

The platform became safer to operate and easier to trust: 100+ users kept access through the migration, dashboards and connections were preserved, stale accounts were removed, and permissions were narrowed without breaking production workflows.

100+ users Preserved access during Superset migration with M365 SSO.
24 -> 5 keys Consolidated active API keys as part of GCP remediation.
10 accounts Removed stale service accounts and orphaned IAM bindings.
Superset v4 to v5 migration M365 SSO and RBAC Automated backups and restore paths Least-privilege IAM

Trust layer

Identity: Microsoft 365 SSO, role mapping, and governed user access.
Recovery: automated backups, retention, and restore procedures for production BI metadata.
Permissions: stale accounts removed, API keys consolidated, BigQuery access scoped by dataset and purpose.
Operations: Terraform-managed infrastructure, Kubernetes deployment, and safer production change paths.

Learning velocity mattered here, but it is not the headline: I came from a non-traditional technical path and used AI as a force multiplier while taking ownership of production systems under pressure.

Golf Genie: Conversational Analytics

Internal production AI analytics app that helped leadership, product, and non-technical teams ask complex business questions without starting from a blank SQL prompt.

AI analytics
Response time Golf-related data questions moved from one to several days to almost instant answers, usually within 30 seconds.
Timezone leverage US and Turkey teams no longer had to wait for a Singapore-based analyst to wake up or become available.
Self-service analysis Business users could ask plain-English questions while still seeing SQL and exportable results.
AI done safely Verified query patterns and curated BigQuery datasets kept the AI useful rather than a black box.
Problem

Important product and subscription questions were trapped behind dashboards, SQL, or analyst backlogs. Business users needed a faster way to ask questions such as renewal and usage behavior by customer vintage, month, and geography.

Built

A FastAPI + Gemini analytics app on Cloud Run, connected to curated BigQuery datasets and designed for internal use by leadership, product, and any team that needed data answers.

Judgment

The app was more than a chatbot connected to a database. It used several dozen analyst-verified query patterns, showed the SQL being used, and made CSV/Excel exports part of the workflow so business follow-up with analysts started from concrete evidence.

Impact

Teams could explore business questions in plain English while still preserving transparency. The generated SQL and exports reduced the friction of moving from a first answer to deeper analysis.

Google Magellan: Export Business Line

Turned advertiser international expansion pain into a scaled Google business line with partner networks, sales enablement, and payments-acceptance workflows.

Business line
Sizing the problem Large advertisers faced 12-18 month international expansion barriers.
Business impact Magellan later supported tens of millions in revenue as a scaled business line.
Customer discovery 200+ interviews turned vague expansion pain into repeatable productized themes.
Sales leverage 7 global payment partners and 50+ trained salespeople made the offer scalable.
Problem

Large advertisers wanted to expand internationally, but market entry could be slowed by payments acceptance, local operating setup, and unclear partner pathways. The pain was real, but it was not yet packaged into a repeatable Google offer.

Built

I used 200+ customer interviews to define Magellan's expansion themes, then built the payments-acceptance pillar: 7 global payment partners, client triage and onboarding paths, and 50+ salespeople trained to consult on expansion blockers.

Judgment

The value was not a generic partnership list. It was a diagnosis process: understand each advertiser's expansion constraint, route them to the right partner or operating fix, and make sales teams capable of spotting the problem early.

Impact

Magellan became a scaled business line for large advertisers, later supporting tens of millions in revenue by helping customers reduce 12-18 month international expansion barriers.

hocudaglasam.com

A public civic-tech product that simplified Serbian diaspora voter registration while keeping sensitive personal data on the user's device.

Public project
Several thousand users Several thousand Serbian citizens abroad registered through the website.
Reusable civic tool The project was reused by other groups in later election cycles.
Privacy by design Registration documents were generated on-device, avoiding server-side PII collection.
Public trust Open-source code and press coverage made the workflow inspectable by citizens and institutions.
Problem

Serbian citizens abroad faced a clumsy registration process: finding the right embassy, downloading and printing forms, filling them manually, scanning documents, and sending everything to the correct address.

Built

An open-source Angular/TypeScript app that generated the registration document client-side and helped users route the completed materials to the relevant embassy or consulate.

Judgment

The core product decision was privacy by architecture. The app did not need server-side PII collection; sensitive data stayed on the user's device, and the code was public so citizens and institutions could inspect the behavior.

Impact

The project became public enough to attract institutional scrutiny and press coverage, which made transparency and clear privacy communication as important as the product workflow itself.
